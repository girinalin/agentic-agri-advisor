# Gray Mold (Botrytis cinerea) Handbook
Gray Mold is a necrotrophic fungus that attacks weakened plant tissue, flowers, and mature fruit.
It thrives in cool, wet climates and spreads by wind-blown spores.
Symptoms:
- Water-soaked spots on flower petals.
- Dusty, gray-brown velvet mold covering infected fruits and stems.
- Stem cankers that can girdle and kill mature crop lines.
Treatment:
- Promptly remove and burn infected plant parts.
- Maintain relative humidity below 85% in greenhouses.
- Spray organic potassium bicarbonate or protective copper-based sprays.
