# Phytophthora infestans Pathology and Late Blight Diagnostics
Late Blight is a highly destructive oomycete disease attacking Solanaceae crops, primarily tomatoes and potatoes.
Unlike true fungi, Phytophthora infestans thrives in high humidity (>90%) and mild temperatures (15-22°C).
Symptoms:
1. Irregular water-soaked spots on leaf tips and margins, rapidly expanding into large brown-black lesions.
2. White downy sporulation visible on leaf undersides under humid conditions.
3. Dark brown lesions on stems, causing systemic collapse.
Control:
- Cultural: Space rows to increase airflow, apply drip irrigation instead of overhead watering, and destroy cull piles.
- Chemical: Apply copper-based organic fungicides preemptively before wet weather patterns set in.
