# Economics of On-Farm Grain Storage
Storing grain on-farm is a strategic financial tool to bypass low spot-market prices during the harvest glut.
Key Considerations:
1. Moisture Control: Grain must be dried to 13-14% moisture to prevent mold and heat damage during storage.
2. Aeration: Run fans during cool nights to equalize bin temperatures and prevent moisture migration.
3. Economic Return: Historically, grain prices recover by 8-12% by late winter. Farmers should compare storage bin maintenance costs against projected price increases to calculate net returns.
